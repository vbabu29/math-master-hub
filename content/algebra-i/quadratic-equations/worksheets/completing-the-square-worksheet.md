## What is Completing the Square?

Completing the square is a method for rewriting a quadratic expression so that it becomes a perfect square plus or minus a constant. This makes some quadratic equations easier to solve and helps connect equations to vertex form.

## How to Complete the Square

1. Move the constant term to the other side.
2. Take half of the coefficient of $x$.
3. Square that number.
4. Add it to both sides of the equation.
5. Rewrite the left side as a square and solve.

### Example

Solve $x^2 + 6x - 7 = 0$

Move the constant:

$x^2 + 6x = 7$

Take half of $6$, which is $3$, and square it to get $9$.

Add $9$ to both sides:

$x^2 + 6x + 9 = 16$

Rewrite:

$(x + 3)^2 = 16$

So $x + 3 = \pm 4$, which gives $x = 1$ or $x = -7$.

## FAQ

**Q: Why do we add the same number to both sides?**  
**A:** Adding the same number to both sides keeps the equation balanced.

**Q: When is completing the square useful?**  
**A:** It is especially helpful when factoring is difficult and when rewriting a quadratic into vertex form.
